# Council: M&A review

## When to invoke

Invoke this council for any acquisition, divestiture, or merger decision. Includes asset purchases of $5M+, full-team acquihires, and joint-venture structures with material capital commitment.

```
boardroom council m-and-a: [decision]
```

## Composition

The default 7-role council. CHRO (people-integration) is decision-shaping in M&A and is already in the default — this preset adds aggregator context that emphasizes the M&A-specific gating questions.

- **CEO** — strategic case, management-attention cost, integration narrative
- **CFO** — deal economics, scenario analysis, post-close cash position, earn-out structure
- **COO** — integration-plan readiness, operational risk, customer-handoff protocols
- **CTO** — technical-systems integration, data migration, platform consolidation
- **General Counsel** — change-of-control clauses, contractual diligence, IP assignment, regulatory approval
- **CHRO** — senior-team retention, compensation harmonization, cultural-integration risk, regretted attrition during integration
- **Board Chair** ⚔ — comparison case (organic build with same capital), motivated-reasoning detection, hostile-activist framing

## Context for the aggregator

This is an M&A council. Pay particular attention to:

- **Change-of-control clauses** on acquired company's customer contracts (often the deal-killer; concentrate review on top-10 customers by ARR)
- **Senior-team retention package design** — equity, cash, role guarantees, non-compete; target 90%+ retention through month 18
- **Cultural / integration risk** in the senior team — CHRO names this; CEO weighs it strategically
- **Synergy realization timeline** — most synergies surface 12-24 months post-close, not in deal-model assumptions
- **Deal economics including earn-out** — total expected cost = headline price + expected earn-out × probability of hitting milestones
- **Comparison case** — what does the same capital deployed organically over the same horizon produce? Board Chair surfaces this if the team has not modeled it
- **Cash position post-close** — runway and working-capital reserve under base, bear, and severe-bear scenarios

## What the synthesis should always answer

1. Is the customer base actually transferable (change-of-control review)?
2. Is the senior team actually retainable through integration (CHRO retention plan)?
3. Has the comparison case (organic build) been modeled at the same level of detail as the acquisition case?
4. Is post-close cash position resilient to a single bad quarter?
5. What's the management-attention opportunity cost on core business growth during 12-18 month integration?
